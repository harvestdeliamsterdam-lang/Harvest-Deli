The Tasting

What you taste, slowly.

I, First

"Warm resin, sun on stone."

An immediate breath of dark wood and forest floor, the smell of the mountain itself in late summer, when the chestnut canopy is heavy with bloom.

II, Body

"Soft amber, slow honey."

The texture takes hold next, a viscosity that pours like a held breath. Wildflower and a clean, gentle sweetness held in the centre of the tongue.

III, Finish

"Mineral, long, golden."

A slow descent into stone and salt. Subtle, almost dry. The kind of finish that lingers in the room long after the spoon has been set down.

Pelion · 950m · Late summer
The Origin

One meadow, one season.

The 2025 harvest comes from a single, south-facing chestnut grove at 950 metres on the slopes of Mount Pelion, where the trees bloom in a narrow window each late summer.

Three hundred and eighty-four jars were drawn from this season. Every one numbered by hand, sealed with black wax, and kept exactly as it left the comb.
